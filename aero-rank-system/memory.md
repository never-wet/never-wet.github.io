# AeroRank Memory

## Design Principles

- Build a vehicle analysis system, not a normal car landing page.
- The car remains the center of gravity in every state.
- Use black, deep charcoal, sharp white type, thin grid lines, ranked identity, scan labels, and concise system copy.
- The visual mood can be inspired by `tbhx.net/en`: load state, ranking language, selector rhythm, bold monochrome hierarchy, and cinematic reveals.
- Adapt the reference into premium automotive analysis. Do not copy characters, story, graphics, or anime styling.

## System UI Rules

- Organize content as states: boot, identity, aerodynamic scan, performance analysis, comparison, final command.
- Use short labels: `AERO RANK`, `FLOW`, `PRESSURE`, `WAKE`, `SCAN`, `CLASS`.
- Data panels must be sparse and precise. No crowded dashboards.
- Avoid ecommerce sections, generic marketing blocks, large unrelated cards, and long paragraphs.
- Buttons and selectors should feel like command surfaces with sharp hover feedback.

## Car Profile Rules

- Porsche 911: icy blue, precision and balance, smooth attached airflow.
- Ferrari SF90: red and magenta, aggression and speed, sharp split high-speed channels.
- Lotus Emira: green and cyan, lightness and control, balanced clean airflow.
- BMW M3: electric blue and white, power and stability, stronger turbulence and wake.
- Each car needs a score, classification, performance profile, aerodynamic profile, and unique flow behavior.

## Motion Rules

- Nothing should simply appear.
- Use boot loading, scan lines, staggered reveals, selector recalculation, camera drift, scroll-triggered state changes, and parallax depth.
- GSAP owns system-level timelines and scroll state. R3F owns continuous 3D motion.
- Honor reduced motion by reducing intensity while preserving state changes.

## Airflow Rules

- Air enters from the left, reads the nose, bends across roof/side/underbody, and exits through a wake zone.
- Airflow must feel directional and engineered, not random decoration.
- Wake behavior changes per car through turbulence, spread, and attachment values.
- Labels should point to real vehicle zones: nose split, roof attachment, side channel, diffuser wake.

## Things To Avoid

- Normal car sales page structure.
- Big hero headline detached from the car.
- Long generic marketing copy.
- Decorative particles that do not imply flow.
- One-note color treatment outside the active car accent.
- UI cards inside UI cards.
